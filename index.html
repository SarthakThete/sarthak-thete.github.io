About Me
Who I Am

I'm an Electronics & Communication Engineering student at MIT WPU, Pune, specializing in embedded systems and edge AI. I thrive at the boundary between hardware and software — where low-level firmware meets real-world sensing and intelligent processing.

My work spans designing IoT pipelines with MQTT, training computer vision models on edge devices like the Raspberry Pi 5, building motor control systems for CNC machines, and programming microcontrollers (STM32, ESP32, Arduino) in Embedded C and C.

I've also had hands-on industrial exposure through the iACE National Academic Immersion Program, where I worked with automotive communication protocols (CAN & LIN), ADAS concepts, and Industry 4.0 workflows.
